Overview

Problem Statement

When faced with the daunting task of deciding their future, many students struggle to understand which career paths align with their interests and strengths, how to build relevant skills, and how to showcase their potential to employers beyond grades or resumes. Despite access to online learning platforms and job portals, students often face three major challenges:

  1. Information Overload: Too many unstructured choices without personalized guidance.
  2. Lack of Ongoing Support: Limited mentorship and motivation during their skill development journey.
  3. Visibility Gap: Employers struggle to see a studentâ€™s growth, leadership potential, and adaptability â€” qualities not reflected in traditional resumes.

This results in uncertainty, mismatched career choices, and missed opportunities for both students and employers.

Solution

SkillTrack is an intelligent web platform that helps students discover and pursue career paths by generating personalized learning plans with recommended courses, certifications, and internships based on their interests and aspirations and offers a professional, data-driven public dashboard that showcases studentsâ€™ growth, achievements, and leadership potential to prospective employers, creating a seamless ecosystem for learning, reflection, and career visibility.

Primary User

The primary user of this website was chosen to be students or general learners who wish to :

  • Explore career options and choose one that aligns with their interests and skills
  • Upskill effectively and in sync with the relevant job market
  • Showcase their potential in a holistic way

User Research

Research Aims

The main motivation behind conducting user research was to validate the following points :

  • Understand the challenges students face in exploring careers, upskilling, and showcasing their potential
  • Confirm whether proposed features like AI mentorship, career pathways, and growth dashboards align with real user expectations
  • Determine which aspects of the platform are most valuable to students and employers to focus design efforts effectively

Research Methods

Interviews

Two in-depth interviews were conducted with university students to understand their career exploration journey. Students shared their struggles with finding relevant mentors in specialized fields, the overwhelming nature of unstructured online learning platforms, and the challenge of showcasing skills that don't fit traditional resume formats.

Survey

12 responses were collected among students aged 19 - 24 scattered across multiple majors and universities that showed 75% of students feel overwhelmed by scattered career information. With average confidence at 3/5, students struggle most with knowing which skills to prioritize and accessing relevant mentorship.

Research Results

Main Pain Points

  1. Information Overload & Lack of Structure
    • Students feel overwhelmed by scattered, unorganized career information
    • No clear prerequisite pathways for learning
    • Difficulty knowing which skills to prioritize or learn first
    • Survey shows "too much information and it feels overwhelming" as a top challenge
  2. Access to Relevant Mentors
    • Hard to find mentors in specific specializations (e.g., computer graphics)
    • Existing mentors often show bias toward their own career paths
    • Older mentors' advice feels outdated for current job market conditions
    • Strong desire for mentors who graduated 5-10 years ago with recent, relevant experience
  3. Skills That Can't Be Showcased
    • Resumes limited to technical achievements, often just one soft skill experience
    • Personal qualities known only through mentors/peers (e.g., "digging deep into research questions")
    • Creative work and hobbies that demonstrate skills but don't fit resume format
    • Blender drawings, personal projects that show capability but have no space

Key Takeaways

  1. Personalized Learning Paths
    • Clear, structured progression of skills/courses
    • Guidance on what to learn and when
    • Understanding prerequisites and dependencies
  2. Better Skill Showcasing
    • Ways to demonstrate soft skills beyond one resume line
    • Platforms to highlight projects and creative work
    • Tools to summarize capabilities holistically
  3. Ideal Mentor
    • Can provide unbiased, holistic views of different specializations
    • Offer personalized, field-specific guidance
    • Help with practical job application strategies, not just skill development

Key User Tasks

From this user research, three key user tasks was decided :

Task 1: Generate a career path

Given a student's interests, aspirations etc generate a list of career path options or generate courses, certifications and internship roles to support a previously decided career path

Task 2: AI Mentor

An AI mentor that can answer personalized career path questions

Task 3: Public profile to be viewed by employers

A public dashboard that can be viewed by potential employers that shows a student's growth and potential

Prototyping

Sketches & Low Fidelity Prototype

Before developing the hifi prototype, I started by creating a series of conceptual sketches to visualize possible layouts and user flows. After completing the sketches, I reviewed them to identify the weak points in terms of usability, clarity, and flow. These insights guided the creation of the low-fidelity prototype, which served as the foundation for the overall hifi prototype.
